name: grill-me
description: Interview the user relentlessly about a plan or design to stress-test it before building. Activate whenever the user wants to grill, pressure-test, poke holes in, challenge, or critically review a plan, design, approach, or proposal before committing to implementation — including phrasings like "grill me", "stress-test this plan", "poke holes in this", or "what am I missing".
---

Interview me relentlessly about every aspect of this plan until we reach a shared understanding. Walk down each branch of the design tree, resolving dependencies between decisions one-by-one. For each question, provide your recommended answer.

Ask the questions one at a time, waiting for feedback on each question before continuing. Asking multiple questions at once is bewildering.

If a question can be answered by exploring the codebase, explore the codebase instead.

## Project style

Before starting, read `project-style.md` (bundled alongside this file) for this
project's grilling preferences (flow, explanation style, and bullet format) and
follow them. That file is the single source of truth for grilling style — do not
restate its contents here.
